# 📊 Telco Customer Churn Analysis — Key Insights

## 🧠 1. Customers Avoid Long-Term Commitment

Customers on month-to-month contracts are far more likely to leave compared to those on yearly plans. The churn rate for these users is roughly **3–4x higher** than customers on long-term contracts.

👉 This clearly shows that when customers are not tied into a commitment, they are much more likely to switch or drop off.

---

## ⏳ 2. The First Year is Critical

Customers in their first 12 months have the highest churn, with nearly **1 out of every 2 new customers leaving**.

👉 This suggests that the **early experience is not strong enough**, and customers are deciding very quickly whether to stay or leave.

---

## 💰 3. Higher Prices = Higher Churn

Customers paying higher monthly charges tend to churn significantly more than those on lower plans. High-paying customers show churn rates close to **35–40%**, while low-paying customers are much more stable.

👉 This indicates that customers may not be seeing enough value for the price they are paying.

---

## 🌐 4. Premium (Fiber) Users Are Surprisingly Less Loyal

Fiber optic users, despite being premium customers, churn more than DSL users. Their churn rate is noticeably higher.

👉 This could mean **higher expectations are not being met**, or pricing for premium services is too aggressive.

---

## 🛠️ 5. Support Services Make a Big Difference

Customers who do not have services like tech support or online security are much more likely to leave. Those who use these services churn at nearly **half the rate**.

👉 This shows that **engagement and support increase stickiness**.

---

## 📉 6. Most Churn Happens Early

A large portion of total churn comes from customers in the early stages (low tenure). In fact, early-stage customers contribute to nearly **half of all churn cases**.

👉 This reinforces that **retention efforts should focus heavily on the initial months** of the customer journey.
